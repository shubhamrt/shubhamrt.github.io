Research

I use connectivity thinking—especially complex networks and spatiotemporal analysis—to understand where environmental systems become vulnerable, and how we can detect change early enough to act.

Download CV Google Scholar

Themes

Dryland degradation & ecogeomorphic connectivity

  • Mapping how water–sediment–vegetation interactions connect (or disconnect) across landscapes.
  • Using network metrics to flag critical pathways and early-warning signals of instability.

Dust source dynamics & wind-driven processes

  • Linking land condition, thresholds, and erodibility to anticipate dust emission risk.
  • Connecting process models with “critical node” ideas to target monitoring and action.

Spatiotemporal rainfall & hydroclimate networks

  • Network-based rainfall reconstruction, gauge-network design, and forecast evaluation.
  • Detecting changes in rainfall patterns across decades and translating them into risk signals.

Interdisciplinary connectivity science

  • Multilayer networks for connectivity in ecology, geomorphology, and coupled human–natural systems.
  • Transferable tools for identifying structure, influence, and thresholds in complex systems.
