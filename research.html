Research

I work on connectivity, thresholds, and stability in environmental systems using complex network theory, spatiotemporal analysis, and process-based modeling. Much of my work sits at the intersection of dryland degradation, dust source dynamics, and hydroclimate networks.

Dryland connectivity

Using networks to map pathways and bottlenecks that shape degradation and recovery.

  • Water–sediment–vegetation connectivity
  • Early-warning signals from structure

Dust source dynamics

Extending connectivity ideas to wind-driven processes and dust emission risk.

  • Erodibility patterns & hotspots
  • Linking models to observations

Rainfall networks

Spatiotemporal structure in rainfall and what it implies for hydrology and monitoring.

  • Network-based rainfall reconstruction
  • Sensor placement & node importance
