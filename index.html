Welcome

I’m a computational geomorphologist and ecohydrologist working at the interface of applied science and environmental management—using networks and models to make connectivity usable for decisions.

About

My work focuses on quantifying hydrologic connectivity and stability in ecogeomorphic systems, with a particular emphasis on understanding land degradation processes in drylands.

I combine network analysis, spatial–temporal methods, and numerical modeling to identify key pathways, critical nodes, and early-warning signals—translating complex dynamics into practical guidance for land and water management.
