Connectivity that guides
resilient drylands

We turn patterns of wind, water, and vegetation into actionable connectivity metrics for restoration, grazing, and risk reduction in drylands.

Wind Connectivity

Quantify where sand and dust can travel across bare interspaces. We map aerodynamic pathways and shrub-sheltering to locate sources, sinks, and breakpoints.

Runoff Connectivity

Detect overland flow paths that link patches to channels. Simple indices flag where microtopography and vegetation gaps amplify water and nutrient export.

Patch & Gap Structure

Measure patch sizes, spacing, and gap chains. Early-warning statistics highlight when small changes in cover can flip systems to eroding states.

Remote Sensing to Metrics

Convert imagery and UAS surveys into connectivity layers—cover, roughness, flow accumulation—to compare treatment options across sites.

Break the Pathways

Test interventions—microcatchments, nurse shrubs, exclosures, grazing plans—by how well they shorten wind and water pathway length.

Simple, Usable Tools

Deliver lightweight indices and dashboards that fit field workflows—clarity first, so decisions can move as fast as the wind and water.
