Research focus

I'm interested in why landscapes tip ‚Äî how grasslands become shrublands, how soils get exposed and eroded, and how small patches of bare ground connect into large, persistent scars. I use network theory to map and quantify these connections, turning messy field data and drone surveys into actionable insight for land managers.

I model how water and sediment move downslope in patchy drylands. By treating every bare patch, shrub, and micro‚Äëchannel as nodes in a network, we can identify critical nodes where intervention has the biggest payoff.
